A quieter stretch, with one big exception: GLM-5.2's open weights landed under MIT. Beyond that, the standout new material was in networking — two sharp ipSpace lab writeups — and in ready-to-use skills, alongside a few practical retrieval-and-eval lessons worth keeping.

## AI Engineering

**[A 9-point eval gain that vanished after deduping train against test](https://dev.to/marcuswwchen/a-9-point-eval-gain-vanished-when-we-deduped-train-against-test-3baj)**  
A team traces a reported 71%-to-80% jump to test rows that had near-duplicates sitting in the training data; once MinHash-based dedup removed the contaminated rows, the real gain was about 1.4 points. They turn the lesson into a CI gate that refuses to ship an eval set when too many rows have a training near-duplicate.  
*dev.to · Jun 15*

## Applied AI Tips

**[Treat retrieval as a sequence of choices, not a reflex](https://dev.to/kseniase/your-rag-stack-is-solving-the-2023-problem-53m8)**  
A critique of the default "chunk, embed, top-k, stuff" RAG pipeline, with five concrete upgrade decisions and the reasoning for each: route on whether to retrieve at all, separate similarity from evidence, preserve structure in tables and code, and gate untrusted sources. A decision framework rather than a tool pitch.  
*dev.to · Jun 16*

**[Let the model write, then overwrite the facts](https://dev.to/rocciavia/how-i-stopped-my-ai-trip-planner-from-inventing-addresses-486o)**  
A clean separation-of-concerns pattern for grounding: retrieve verified facts, let the model produce the prose and structure, then overwrite the factual fields — addresses, coordinates — with authoritative values using a typed output schema, so the model never owns the facts. Kills a whole class of hallucinations.  
*dev.to · Jun 16*

## Networking

**[EVPN asymmetric IRB: the anycast-gateway ARP trap](https://blog.ipspace.net/2026/06/arp-issues-evpn-anycast-unicast/)**  
Ivan Pepelnjak walks through how shared anycast MAC/IP gateways can silently break ARP resolution in EVPN asymmetric IRB, why the reply never comes back, and the fix — with a runnable lab topology to reproduce it.  
*ipSpace · Jun 17*

**[SR-MPLS over OSPFv2: how the IGP encoding differs from IS-IS](https://blog.ipspace.net/2026/06/sr-mpls-ospf/)**  
A hands-on lab walkthrough showing that OSPF needs a separate opaque LSA for every piece of segment-routing state — global block, prefix-to-node-SID, each adjacency SID — versus the compact IS-IS TLVs, with CLI verification of the label bindings.  
*ipSpace · Jun 15*

**[LatencyMON, rebuilt](https://labs.ripe.net/author/stephen_suess_1/latencymon-rebuilt-safer-faster-and-more-connected/)**  
RIPE Labs details a ground-up rewrite of its RIPE Atlas latency-visualization tool: it groups probes by geographic distance into min/median/max bands, scales opacity by packet loss, and now covers DNS, TLS and HTTP measurements rather than just ping and traceroute.  
*RIPE Labs · Jun 16*

**[DNS query duplication, measured](https://blog.apnic.net/2026/06/17/dns-query-duplication/)**  
APNIC digs into why resolvers sometimes send the same DNS query more than once — what causes the duplication, how often it happens, and what it means for authoritative-server operators and anyone measuring DNS. A data-grounded operations read.  
*APNIC · Jun 17*

## AI Deals & Free Tools

**[GLM-5.2 — frontier-level open weights under MIT](https://huggingface.co/zai-org/GLM-5.2)**  
Zhipu / Z.ai's GLM-5.2 landed its actual model weights on Hugging Face under the MIT license — a 1M-token context, two reasoning-effort levels, and faster speculative decoding. It was announced earlier in June, but the downloadable open weights — the part you can self-host — only dropped this week, putting frontier-level capability within reach of anyone with the GPUs to run it.  
*Hugging Face · MIT · weights Jun 17*
