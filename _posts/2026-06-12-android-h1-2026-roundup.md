The whole half-year in Android, in one place: the fight over sideloading and developer verification that dominated the spring, the apps actually worth installing across the six months, and the tooling catching up to agent-driven development.

## The fight over sideloading

**[Keep Android Open](https://f-droid.org/2026/02/20/twif.html)**  
F-Droid's rallying post as Google's developer-verification plans came into view — the clearest statement of what's at stake for sideloading and independent app distribution.  
*F-Droid · Feb 20*

**[An open letter opposing Android developer verification](https://f-droid.org/2026/02/24/open-letter-opposing-developer-verification.html)**  
The formal community response, laying out the specific objections to mandatory developer verification.  
*F-Droid · Feb 24*

**[Google details its 24-hour process to sideload unverified apps](https://arstechnica.com/gadgets/2026/03/google-details-new-24-hour-process-to-sideload-unverified-android-apps/)**  
Google's described mechanism for installing unverified apps — the concrete policy the whole debate is really about.  
*Ars Technica · Mar 19*

## Apps worth installing

**[DoNotNotify — log and intelligently block notifications](https://donotnotify.com/)**  
A genuinely useful utility that captures and selectively silences notifications — the rare app that reduces noise rather than adding it.  
*Show HN · Jan 5*

**[ElixirBrowser — an Android Chromium fork with extension support](https://github.com/SF-FLAM/ElixirBrowser)**  
A community browser bringing real extension support to Android, for people who want desktop-style browsing on a phone.  
*GitHub · Jan 9*

**[Clojure-Android — native Clojure on your phone](https://github.com/clj-android)**  
A resurrection of native Clojure development on Android — niche, but a notable piece of language tooling.  
*GitHub · Mar 26*

**[Conduit — a local-first SSH, Mosh and SFTP client](https://github.com/gwitko/Conduit)**  
A clean terminal companion that keeps your sessions and keys on the device rather than in someone's cloud.  
*GitHub · Jun 11*

**[PennyWise — private, on-device expense tracking](https://f-droid.org/en/packages/com.pennywiseai.tracker/)**  
Turns bank transaction texts into a tidy spending timeline, categorised entirely on the device. (Needs broad message access by design.)  
*F-Droid · Jun*

**[Basepanel — a database editor for your phone](https://www.basepanel.com/)**  
Edit Postgres, MySQL and SQLite databases without reaching for a laptop.  
*Show HN · Jun 11*

**[Tapflow — self-hosted mobile-simulator streaming](https://github.com/jo-duchan/tapflow)**  
Stream a running mobile simulator to your device — handy for QA on screen sizes you don't own.  
*GitHub · Jun 10*

**[80bee — ADB tools without a computer](https://github.com/Endda/80bee-app)**  
Brings the Android debugging toolkit directly onto the device for tinkerers.  
*GitHub · Jun 7*

**[MTProto Hub — manage Telegram proxies](https://github.com/whitecristafer/mtprotohub)**  
A clean single-purpose tool for collecting, testing and using MTProto proxies.  
*GitHub · Jun 10*
