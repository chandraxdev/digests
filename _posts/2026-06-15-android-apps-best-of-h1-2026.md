Six months of Android, distilled to what actually earned a place on a phone. Two threads ran through the half: a genuine fight over who gets to install what — Google's developer-verification push and the F-Droid-led pushback on sideloading — and a quiet, steady stream of local-first tools that keep your data on the device instead of in someone's cloud. Below are the standouts, weighted toward privacy, open source, and things that do one job well.

## Developer & terminal tools

**[A local-first terminal companion (Conduit)](https://github.com/gwitko/Conduit)**  
A clean SSH, Mosh and SFTP client that keeps your sessions and keys on the device rather than in someone's cloud — a solid pick if you manage servers from your phone.  
*GitHub · Jun 11*

**[A database editor for your pocket (Basepanel)](https://www.basepanel.com/)**  
Poke at Postgres, MySQL and SQLite databases straight from your phone, no laptop required. Worth checking how it handles credentials before you wire it to anything sensitive.  
*Show HN · Jun 11*

**[Test on a phone you don't own (Tapflow)](https://github.com/jo-duchan/tapflow)**  
Self-hosted streaming of a running mobile simulator to your device — handy for QA across screen sizes you don't have hardware for.  
*GitHub · Jun 10*

**[ADB without a computer (80bee)](https://github.com/Endda/80bee-app)**  
Brings the Android debugging toolkit directly onto the device, so tinkerers can run developer commands without plugging into a PC.  
*GitHub · Jun 7*

**[Build Android apps from any coding agent](https://android-developers.googleblog.com/2026/04/build-android-apps-3x-faster-using-any-agent.html)**  
Google's own tooling for driving Android builds from any coding agent — the platform meeting the agentic-development moment.  
*Android Developers · Apr 16*

## Privacy & local-first

**[Expense tracking that never leaves your phone (PennyWise)](https://f-droid.org/en/packages/com.pennywiseai.tracker/)**  
Turns your bank's transaction texts into a tidy, searchable spending timeline, with categorisation running entirely on-device — no cloud, no sign-up. The honest caveat: it needs broad access to your messages to read those texts in the first place.  
*F-Droid · Jun*

**[Log and intelligently block notifications (DoNotNotify)](https://donotnotify.com/)**  
A utility that captures and selectively silences notifications — the rare app that cuts noise instead of adding to it.  
*Show HN · Jan 5*

**[A Chromium fork with real extension support (ElixirBrowser)](https://github.com/SF-FLAM/ElixirBrowser)**  
A community browser bringing desktop-style extension support to Android, for people who want a fuller browsing setup on a phone.  
*GitHub · Jan 9*

## Utilities & ecosystem

**[Manage Telegram proxies (MTProto Hub)](https://github.com/whitecristafer/mtprotohub)**  
A clean single-purpose tool for collecting, testing and using MTProto proxies — niche, but exactly what you want if you depend on them for connectivity.  
*GitHub · Jun 10*

**[Keep Android Open — the case against locking down sideloading](https://f-droid.org/2026/02/20/twif.html)**  
F-Droid's rallying post as Google's developer-verification plans came into focus — the clearest statement of what's at stake for sideloading and independent app distribution.  
*F-Droid · Feb 20*

**[Google's 24-hour process for sideloading unverified apps](https://arstechnica.com/gadgets/2026/03/google-details-new-24-hour-process-to-sideload-unverified-android-apps/)**  
The concrete policy at the centre of that debate, laid out plainly — the rules everyone is actually arguing about.  
*Ars Technica · Mar 19*
