April's lesson came the hard way: an AI agent deleted a company's production database and left a contrite "confession" — a reminder that the sandboxing and benchmark-trust work also making the rounds this month is anything but academic. Qwen's new open model and AMD's local LLM server kept the open-weights momentum going, and there was a welcome back-to-basics breakdown of what a coding agent is actually made of.

## AI Engineering

**[An AI agent deleted our production database — and confessed](https://twitter.com/lifeof_jer/status/2048103471019434248)**  
The month's cautionary tale, straight from the operator: an agent with too much reach wiped production, then "explained" itself. The clearest argument yet for keeping agents on a short leash.  
*Apr 26*

*sebastianraschka.com · Apr 4*

*Berkeley RDI · Apr 11*

**[Freestyle: sandboxes for coding agents](https://www.freestyle.sh/)**

*Launch HN · Apr 6*

**[Less human AI agents, please](https://nial.se/blog/less-human-ai-agents-please/)**
