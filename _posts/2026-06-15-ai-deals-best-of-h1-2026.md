A look back at the genuinely free and open finds of the past six months — not this week's news, but the offers and tools from across the first half of 2026 that are still worth grabbing. It was a strong stretch for open weights, with frontier-class families like DeepSeek V4 and GLM-5 shipping under permissive licences you can actually download and run. Below are the picks that hold up: open models you own, free inference tiers you can claim today, and self-hostable tools with no trial clock.

## Open-weight model releases

**[DeepSeek V4 lands with open weights and a million-token context](https://huggingface.co/deepseek-ai/DeepSeek-V4-Pro)**  
A frontier-class mixture-of-experts family (V4-Pro and the lighter V4-Flash) released MIT-licensed on Hugging Face with a 1M-token context — free to download, self-host, and use commercially. Flash is realistically self-hostable; Pro is servable cheaply.  
*Hugging Face · Apr 2026*

**[GLM-5 — a 744B open-weight model under MIT](https://huggingface.co/zai-org/GLM-5)**  
Zhipu's 744B-parameter (40B active) mixture-of-experts, released under the MIT license on Hugging Face, with cheap hosted access via Z.ai and OpenRouter if you lack the GPUs. One of the largest fully-MIT open-weight models to date.  
*Hugging Face · Feb 2026*

**[Kimi K2.6 — a 1T-parameter open-weights coding model](https://huggingface.co/moonshotai/Kimi-K2.6)**  
Moonshot AI's 1T-param mixture-of-experts (32B active, 256K context, natively multimodal and agentic) under a Modified MIT License — code and weights both free to download. A strong open alternative to the backends behind paid coding agents.  
*Hugging Face · Apr 2026*

**[Qwen3.6 — the Qwen sizes you can actually self-host](https://github.com/QwenLM/Qwen3.6)**  
Alibaba's Qwen3.6-27B and 35B-A3B shipped under Apache 2.0 on Hugging Face and ModelScope — free to download, fine-tune, and run on prosumer GPUs. The flagship Qwen3.6-Max is closed; these mid-size tiers are the free path.  
*GitHub · Apr 2026*

## Free inference & credits

**[Cerebras is giving away a million tokens a day, no card required](https://www.cerebras.ai/pricing)**  
The free developer tier grants a reported 1,000,000 tokens/day across hosted models (Llama Scout, Qwen3, gpt-oss-120B) at very high throughput, no credit card and no waitlist — enough to run real workloads at zero cost, with the usual per-minute rate caps.  
*Cerebras · 2026*

**[Mistral's free "Experiment" plan — just a phone number](https://intercom.help/mistral-ai/en/articles/14841555-how-can-i-try-the-api-for-free-with-the-experiment-plan)**  
Free API access to Mistral's open and proprietary models (including Large and Codestral) with no credit card, just a verified phone number. Worth heeding the plan's own caveat: Experiment requests may be used to train Mistral's models, so keep sensitive data out.  
*Mistral*

**[OpenRouter — one key, dozens of free models](https://openrouter.ai/models?max_price=0)**  
The free tier exposes a couple of dozen `:free` models (DeepSeek R1, Llama 3.3 70B, Qwen3 Coder, Gemini Flash and more) with no credit card. A one-time, non-expiring $10 top-up permanently raises the daily request cap — a cheap, simple way to try many open models before committing.  
*OpenRouter*

**[Groq — fast free inference on email sign-up](https://console.groq.com/docs/rate-limits)**  
Instant API access to fast open models like Llama 3.3 70B with published per-model free limits, no credit card — just email or Google sign-up. Reliable enough for light production and a good match for agents and chatbots.  
*Groq*

## Self-hostable tools you own

**[OpenClaw — a self-hosted personal assistant you actually own](https://github.com/openclaw/openclaw)**  
The open-source breakout of the half-year: an MIT-licensed personal AI agent that runs on your own devices, connecting LLMs to 50+ integrations with data that never leaves your machine. Free to self-host; bring your own model keys.  
*GitHub · Jan 2026*

**[Dify — a free, self-hostable platform for AI apps and agents](https://github.com/langgenius/dify)**  
An Apache 2.0 platform for building agentic workflows — visual builder, retrieval, model routing — free to self-host as a zero-cost alternative to paid LLM-app platforms. Bring your own model keys and run the whole stack yourself.  
*GitHub*

**[OpenHands — a free autonomous coding agent you can run locally](https://github.com/All-Hands-AI/OpenHands)**  
An MIT-licensed software-engineering agent that edits code, runs commands, browses and calls APIs — free to self-host and point at local models via Ollama. Pair it with one of the open-weight models above for a zero-cost agentic-coding stack.  
*GitHub*
