A pick of the standout reading across our beats so far this year — the pieces worth keeping, drawn from across the first half of 2026 rather than any single week. Open weights had a strong half-year (DeepSeek V4, GLM-5 and others under permissive licences), the sharpest engineering writing sat a layer below the model in harnesses and evaluation, and there was solid work in routing security, open-sourced infrastructure, and privacy-minded Android tools.

## AI Engineering

**[The harness is the product — a survey of where agent quality really lives](https://labs.beconfident.app/papers/harness-engineering-survey)**  
A sharp synthesis of how production AI systems actually win: not the model, but the loop, context, tools, safety and evals around it — with a clear taxonomy and an open tool to keep a knowledge base from going stale.  
*BeConfident Labs · Jun 12*

**[You fixed the rate limits — now your agent fails quietly](https://dev.to/p0rt/you-fixed-the-rate-limits-now-your-agent-fails-quietly-3keo)**  
A genuinely useful read on why retries, fallbacks and caches buy uptime at the cost of correctness, and how to separate the two with idempotency keys and trust-tagging across a chain of steps.  
*dev.to · Jun 11*

**[Same 15 models, one afternoon, big gains — by changing only the harness](http://blog.can.ac/2026/02/12/the-harness-problem/)**  
A vivid demonstration that the scaffolding around a model often matters more than the model itself.  
*blog.can.ac*

**[We replaced RAG with a virtual filesystem for our docs assistant](https://www.mintlify.com/blog/how-we-built-a-virtual-filesystem-for-our-assistant)**  
A refreshing counter-take: instead of vector search, let the agent browse docs like files. Practical write-up with the trade-offs.  
*Mintlify*

**[How attackers poison the documents your RAG system trusts](https://aminrj.com/posts/rag-document-poisoning/)**  
A clear look at a real and under-discussed attack surface, with concrete defences.  
*aminrj.com*

## AI Deals & Free Tools

**[DeepSeek V4 lands with open weights and a million-token context](https://huggingface.co/deepseek-ai/DeepSeek-V4-Pro)**  
A flagship-class model released under MIT — genuinely useful if you want frontier capability you can self-host.  
*Hugging Face*

**[GLM-5 — a 744B open-weight model under MIT](https://huggingface.co/zai-org/GLM-5)**  
Another serious open release worth a look for self-hosting or fine-tuning.  
*Hugging Face*

**[OpenClaw — a self-hosted personal assistant you actually own](https://github.com/openclaw/openclaw)**  
MIT-licensed and self-hostable, the breakout of the half-year for people who'd rather not rent their assistant.  
*GitHub*

**[Cerebras is giving away a million tokens a day, no card required](https://www.cerebras.ai/pricing)**  
A generous free inference tier if you want fast tokens to experiment with.  
*Cerebras*

## Networking

**[Cloudflare: enforce the first AS in BGP paths and shut a hijack class](https://blog.cloudflare.com/enforce-first-as-bgp/)**  
A concrete, deployable routing-security control and the exact forged-path failure it prevents.  
*Cloudflare*

**[Scaling Akvorado's routing store with sharding](https://vincent.bernat.ch/en/blog/2026-akvorado-rib-sharding)**  
Real scaling engineering for flow/telemetry pipelines from the tool's own author.  
*vincent.bernat.ch*

**[Goodbye, leaf-and-spine networks?](https://blog.ipspace.net/2026/06/goodbye-leaf-spine-networks/)**  
A thoughtful challenge to the default data-centre fabric of the last decade.  
*ipSpace*

**[Stop designing route reflectors like it's 2005](https://medium.com/@herve.hildenbrand/stop-designing-route-reflectors-like-its-2005-part-2-the-fast-twitch-muscle-a939fcd8d23d)**  
Opinionated, current guidance on modern iBGP route-reflector placement and scaling.  
*Medium*

## Applied AI Tips

**[Anthropic on context engineering — finding the smallest set of high-signal tokens](https://www.anthropic.com/engineering/effective-context-engineering-for-ai-agents)**  
The canonical framing for what to put in (and keep out of) an agent's context, with practical patterns.  
*Anthropic*

**[The LLM evals FAQ everyone keeps sharing](https://hamel.dev/blog/posts/evals-faq/)**  
Hamel Husain's no-nonsense answers to the questions teams actually have about evaluating LLM systems.  
*hamel.dev*

**[The comprehensive guide to building agents](https://huyenchip.com/2025/01/07/agents.html)**  
Chip Huyen's deep, vendor-neutral overview — still the best single starting point.  
*huyenchip.com*

**[A pragmatic guide to LLM evals for developers](https://newsletter.pragmaticengineer.com/p/evals)**  
Run evals at three points — offline, in production, and pre-merge — cheaply and meaningfully.  
*Pragmatic Engineer*

**[Eight practical tips for writing agent skills](https://www.philschmid.de/agent-skills-tips)**  
Concrete, field-tested advice for getting more out of skills.  
*philschmid.de*

## Android Apps

**[A proper terminal companion — Conduit](https://github.com/gwitko/Conduit)**  
A clean, local-first SSH, Mosh and SFTP client that keeps your sessions and keys on the device.  
*GitHub · Jun 11*

**[Databases in your pocket — Basepanel](https://www.basepanel.com/)**  
A straightforward editor for Postgres, MySQL and SQLite from your phone.  
*Show HN · Jun 11*

**[Test on a phone you don't have — Tapflow](https://github.com/jo-duchan/tapflow)**  
Self-hosted streaming of a running mobile simulator to your device — handy for QA.  
*GitHub · Jun 10*

**[Money tracking that never leaves your phone — PennyWise](https://f-droid.org/en/packages/com.pennywiseai.tracker/)**  
Turns bank texts into a tidy spending timeline, categorised entirely on-device. (Needs broad message access by design.)  
*F-Droid*

**[ADB without a computer — 80bee](https://github.com/Endda/80bee-app)**  
Brings the Android debugging toolkit straight onto the device for tinkerers.  
*GitHub · Jun 7*

**[Proxy wrangling for Telegram — MTProto Hub](https://github.com/whitecristafer/mtprotohub)**  
A clean single-purpose tool for collecting, testing and using MTProto proxies.  
*GitHub · Jun 10*
