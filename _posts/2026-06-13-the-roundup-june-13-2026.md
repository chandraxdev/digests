A broad issue this week. Open weights had a strong run — DeepSeek V4 and GLM-5 both landed under permissive licences — while the sharpest engineering writing sat a layer below the model, in harnesses and evaluation. In the networks, Cloudflare published a deployable fix for a BGP hijack class and Ericsson reported real gains from a Massive MIMO trial; on the open-source front a cross-platform mobile framework went free and Microsoft open-sourced durable execution in Postgres; and there's a small crop of genuinely new, privacy-minded Android tools to close.

## AI Engineering

**[The harness is the product — a survey of where agent quality really lives](https://labs.beconfident.app/papers/harness-engineering-survey)**  
A sharp synthesis of how production AI systems actually win: not the model, but the loop, context, tools, safety and evals around it — with a clear taxonomy and an open tool to keep a knowledge base from going stale.  
*BeConfident Labs · Jun 12*

**[You fixed the rate limits — now your agent fails quietly](https://dev.to/p0rt/you-fixed-the-rate-limits-now-your-agent-fails-quietly-3keo)**  
A genuinely useful read on why retries, fallbacks and caches buy uptime at the cost of correctness, and how to separate the two with idempotency keys and trust-tagging across a chain of steps.  
*dev.to · Jun 11*

**[Same 15 models, one afternoon, big gains — by changing only the harness](http://blog.can.ac/2026/02/12/the-harness-problem/)**  
A vivid demonstration that the scaffolding around a model often matters more than the model itself.  
*blog.can.ac*

**[We replaced RAG with a virtual filesystem for our docs assistant](https://www.mintlify.com/blog/how-we-built-a-virtual-filesystem-for-our-assistant)**  
A refreshing counter-take: instead of vector search, let the agent browse docs like files. Practical write-up with the trade-offs.  
*Mintlify*

**[How attackers poison the documents your RAG system trusts](https://aminrj.com/posts/rag-document-poisoning/)**  
A clear look at a real and under-discussed attack surface, with concrete defences.  
*aminrj.com*

## 5G / 6G RAN Optimization

**[A traffic-steering app that added ~13% throughput in a multi-vendor trial](https://rimedolabs.com/blog/the-oran-whitepaper-2026-ran-automation-with-smo-and-rapps/)**  
Measured gains across 17 cells and 80 emulated users, with the biggest wins at the cell edge — exactly the kind of field evidence that's usually missing.  
*Rimedo Labs*

**[Cutting radio energy with a learning controller, tested over the air](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.09206)**  
A reinforcement-learning app for radio-unit energy optimisation, evaluated on a real 5G open-RAN testbed rather than just simulation.  
*arXiv*

**[Ericsson + KT mid-band Massive MIMO trial — large throughput jumps over LTE](https://www.ericsson.com/ko/press-releases/2/2026/ericsson-and-kt-advance-toward-ai-powered-6g-with-successful-mid-band-fdd-massive-mimo-trial-in-korea)**  
A concrete operator trial with stated figures on the road toward 6G.  
*Ericsson*

**[When two control apps fight: a conflict-mitigation framework for O-RAN](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2503.11566)**  
Addresses a real failure mode — competing apps issuing conflicting instructions — with a testbed evaluation.  
*arXiv*
