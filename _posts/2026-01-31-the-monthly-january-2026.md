January's defining story straddled two of our beats: a BGP anomaly during Venezuela's nationwide blackout, dissected by Cloudflare and independent researchers. In AI engineering, the arrival of Opus 4.5 reset expectations for how an agent behaves, and a small wave of tooling appeared to run coding agents safely — sandboxes, sudo-isolation, and self-hosted agents you own.

## AI Engineering

**[Opus 4.5 is not the agent experience we were used to](https://burkeholland.github.io/posts/opus-4-5-change-everything/)**  
A widely-read practitioner account of how the new model changed day-to-day agentic coding — less babysitting, more genuine autonomy.  
*burkeholland.github.io · Jan 6*

**[Moltworker: a self-hosted personal AI agent](https://blog.cloudflare.com/moltworker-self-hosted-ai-agent/)**  
Cloudflare's take on an assistant you run yourself rather than rent — a notable entry in the "own your agent" trend.  
*Cloudflare · Jan 29*

**[The electricity use of AI coding agents](https://www.simonpcouch.com/blog/2026-01-20-cc-impact/)**  
A rare measured look at the energy footprint of running coding agents, with real numbers rather than hand-waving.  
*simonpcouch.com · Jan 20*

**[Amla Sandbox: a WASM shell sandbox for agents](https://github.com/amlalabs/amla-sandbox)**  
A lightweight way to give an agent a bash shell without giving it your machine — sandboxed execution as a first-class concern.  
*GitHub · Jan 30*

**[Yolobox: run coding agents with full sudo, safely](https://github.com/finbarr/yolobox)**  
Isolation for autonomous coding agents so they can have real privileges without nuking your home directory.  
*GitHub · Jan 12*
